CARDBOARD WASTE

  • General Statement

    • Waste is a substance or material that comes from human activities. Scientifically, waste is the remaining material that no longer has economic value. Waste can be divided into three types: gas, liquid, and solid. One example of solid wate is cardboard waste.

  • Description

    • Cardboard waste is a packaging material made of paper or layered cardboard that is commonly used for wrapping goods or products. This type of waste usually comes from food and beverage packaging, shoe boxes, or other household items. Cardboard is classified as organic waste because it can decompose naturally, although sometimes it needs certain processes to speed up decomposition.

    Cardboard waste can be easily found around us since cardboard if often used to wrap food, drinks, and various other products. Despite being waste, cardboard still has many benefits, both individually and generally. It can be reudes as raw material for making crafts, decorations, or useful recycled products.

  • Benefits

    • Recycling cardboard waste helps reduce the amount of waste that piles up in the environment. The recycling process can turn waste into new, valuable products such as tissue boxes, or wall clocks. In addition, recycling cardboard waste not only helps protect the environment but also provides economic benefits for those who process it.

  • Conclusion

    • Cardboard waste is one of the environmental problems that must be taken seriously. However, because it is easy to recycle and has good water resistance, cardboard can be processed into many beneficial products. Managing cardboard waste properly can raise public awarenesss to recycle, create interesting crafts, and even open job opportunities to improve community welfare.
